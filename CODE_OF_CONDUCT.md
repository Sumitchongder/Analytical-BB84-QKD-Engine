# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Analytical BB84 QKD Engine project is committed to fostering an open,
respectful, and inclusive environment for researchers, developers,
students, and contributors interested in quantum cryptography and secure
communication systems.

We value collaboration, academic integrity, and constructive dialogue,
especially in interdisciplinary domains such as quantum computing,
cryptography, and visualization.

---

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Treat all community members with respect and professionalism
- Engage in constructive, technical, and evidence-based discussions
- Be considerate of diverse backgrounds, experience levels, and viewpoints
- Credit prior work appropriately and avoid plagiarism
- Maintain scientific and ethical integrity in all contributions

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- Harassment, discrimination, or offensive language
- Personal attacks, intimidation, or derogatory remarks
- Misrepresentation of results or academic dishonesty
- Publishing private or sensitive information without consent
- Deliberate disruption of discussions or project development

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project-related spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories, issues, and pull requests
- Project documentation and discussions
- Hackathon-related communication channels
- Public presentations or derivative works referencing this project

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers reserve the right to take appropriate action in response
to violations, including:

- Content removal
- Warnings or temporary restrictions
- Permanent exclusion from project participation

All actions will be taken fairly and proportionately.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, please report it
privately to the project maintainer.

Reports will be handled confidentially and respectfully.

---

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is informed by the Contributor Covenant and adapted
for academic and research-focused open-source projects.
